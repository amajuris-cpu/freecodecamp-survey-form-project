Movie Review: If Beale Street Could Talk

A critically acclaimed 2018 romantic drama film directed by Barry Jenkins, based on James Baldwin’s 1974 novel of the same name. It is symultaneously heartbreaking and beautiful to witness the evolution of Tish and Fonny's relationship from warm childhood friendship to intimate heartfelt lovers and wide-eyed parents. All of this beauty is juxtaposed with the tragedy of systemic racism and police corruption that they survive through with the strength of their family. James Laxton and Nicholas Britell give capture and serenade all the beautiful heartbreak with their cinematography and score respectively. Definitely an all-time favourite, and we can expect nothing less from a Baldwin story, but we are grateful justice was served by Jenkin's direction.

Movie Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐☆(9.5/10)
